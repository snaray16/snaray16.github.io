Implantable Ultrasound for Recurrent Brain Tumor Detection, Team Lead

I led a CBID (Center for Bioengineering, Innovation and Design) design team in the R&D of an implantable ultrasound device for recurrent brain tumor detection.

Recurrent brain tumors are often hard to treat because they grow aggressively and asymptomatically, and are detected too late.

My responsibilities:

    AmbuMate: Enabling Patient Ambulation in the Pediatric Intensive Care Unit, Engineer --> Team Lead

    Souped-up walker helps get pediatric ICU patients back on their feet

    Student-Built Walker to Help Pediatric Patients Get Moving

    A Project That's Going to Help People

    Core Need

    Patient ambulation is crucial to proper recovery for patients with extended stays in the intensive care unit. However, difficulties with managing the patient's care during ambulation make ambulation difficult. Current methods for ambulation are inefficient, unsafe, and therefore infrequently done.

    Our Solution

    By working with doctors and nurses in the Johns Hopkins Hospital's Pediatric Intensive Care Unit, as well as engineers from the Whiting School of Engineering at Johns Hopkins, we developed the AmbuMate: a device that makes patient ambulation safe and easy.

    • Transports all necessary patient equipment
    • Novel wire and tube management system
    • Multiple support methods for patient fatigue
    • Attaches to the patient's native IV pole
    • Integrates seamlessly into clinical workflows
    • Requires only one nurse for effective use

    In an IRB study conducted at Johns Hopkins Hospital, 95% of nurses felt AmbuMate would significantly improve ambulation.
